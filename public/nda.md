### 1. Purpose

The Owner intends to disclose proprietary data, software code, system configurations, or business metrics (the "Confidential Information") to the Recipient solely for the purpose of evaluating, designing, developing, optimizing, or maintaining technical systems and software solutions (the "Permitted Use").

### 2. Definition of Confidential Information

"Confidential Information" refers to any proprietary information disclosed by Owner to Recipient, whether orally, visually, or in tangible/digital form, including but not limited to:

* Source code, database schemas, API configurations, algorithms, and architectural designs.
* User data, customer lists, business metrics, and financial records.
* Access credentials, private keys, server logs, and environment configurations.

### 3. Obligations of Recipient

The Recipient agrees to:

* Hold all Confidential Information in strict confidence.
* Restrict access to the Information solely as necessary for the Permitted Use.
* Implement commercially reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect Confidential Information from unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

### 4. Exclusions

Confidential Information does not include information that:

* Is or becomes publicly known through no breach of this Agreement by the Recipient.
* Was already known to the Recipient prior to disclosure, as proven by written records.
* Is independently developed by the Recipient without any use of or reference to the Owner’s Confidential Information.

### 5. Term and Survival

This Agreement governs all disclosures made between the Parties starting on the Effective Date. The Recipient's duty to protect the Confidential Information shall survive the completion of services and remain in effect for a period of **3 years**, except for proprietary source code and trade secrets, which shall remain confidential indefinitely.

### 6. Return or Destruction of Data

Upon written request by the Owner, or automatically within 30 days following the termination of services, the Recipient shall securely delete, purge, or return all Confidential Information in Recipient’s possession or control, including copies stored on local devices, staging environments, and active systems.

To the extent Confidential Information is retained in encrypted backups, archival systems, or disaster recovery systems maintained in the ordinary course of business, Recipient shall not be required to immediately delete such copies, provided they remain protected under this Agreement and are not restored or used except as required for backup or recovery purposes.

### 7. Governing Law

This Agreement shall be governed by, and construed in accordance with, the laws of the State of Oklahoma, without giving effect to any principles of conflicts of law.
